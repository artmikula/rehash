How it works

Every video carries two signatures: a cryptographic hash over its raw bytes, and a perceptual fingerprint derived from spatio-temporal feature vectors that modern content-ID systems use to match re-encoded uploads.

REHASH runs your clip through a multi-stage perceptual transform pipeline — stochastic chroma rotation, sub-pixel spatial reframing, adaptive bitrate quantization, and temporal resampling — calibrated to drift both signatures past detection thresholds while preserving viewer-visible quality.
